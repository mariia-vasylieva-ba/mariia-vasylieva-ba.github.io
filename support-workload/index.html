Form3 · Data + Python + process redesign

Reducing Engineering Support Load

I analysed how 9 engineering teams at Form3 were spending their time on reactive support work. For one team, I redesigned the cross-team request workflow end-to-end, moving from Slack chaos to a GitHub-based system with templates, SLAs, and automation.

80% → 20% one team's engineering time spent on support work, before → after (self-reported)
4,000+ Slack support queries analysed with a Python + Pandas pipeline
2 hrs from 2 months analysis time per team once the pipeline was built

The problem

Form3 was growing rapidly, and engineering teams kept flagging communication and workflow problems with other teams at retrospectives and team-building events. A previous BA attempt — shadowing team leaders for two weeks — produced some signal, but not enough to act on.

My task was to quantify how engineering teams actually spent their time, identify the biggest sources of reactive work, and design changes that would free up engineering capacity.

The problem was known but unquantified.

What I did

  1. Requested historical support-channel data across 9 engineering teams — 4,000+ Slack queries as the raw dataset.
  2. Built a Python + Pandas analysis pipeline to categorise and quantify the support load per team. An engineer's initial script was the starting point; I rewrote and extended it substantially to answer the questions I needed.
  3. Identified the biggest team-to-team sources of reactive work.
  4. For one team, redesigned the cross-team request workflow end-to-end: replaced ad-hoc Slack requests with a GitHub-based system using templates, SLAs, and automation.
  5. Scaled the analysis pipeline itself: the first team took ~2 months of manual work; by the end, each of the remaining 8 teams took ~2 hours to analyse.

What changed

  • For the redesigned team, support work dropped from approximately 80% to 20% of engineering time — measured by engineer self-reported time-per-request before and after.
  • The analysis approach became reusable across the department: from 2 months for the first team to 2 hours per team.
