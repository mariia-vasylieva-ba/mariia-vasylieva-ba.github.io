KYC Configuration Across 20+ Countries

I worked on a KYC questionnaire platform for a global tier-1 bank, configuring the underlying BPMN engine to handle country-specific KYC processes across 20+ LATAM and APAC jurisdictions. Where the platform couldn't handle a regional requirement, I wrote the technical specifications for engineering to extend it.

20+ jurisdictions configured, each with its own flow, fields, validation, and handoffs
2 regions of stakeholders — LATAM and APAC — supplying the requirements
1 BPMN platform carrying every country's KYC process

The problem

A global tier-1 bank operated KYC processes that differed by jurisdiction. Each country's regime imposed its own questionnaire flow, fields, validation, and handoffs — and the bank ran all of it on a BPMN-driven questionnaire platform that had to be configured per country.

My task was to configure the BPMN engine to handle country-specific KYC processes across 20+ LATAM and APAC jurisdictions, and to capture requirements from regional stakeholders to get each country's process right.

What I did

  1. Worked with LATAM and APAC regional stakeholders to elicit each jurisdiction's KYC requirements.
  2. Configured the BPMN questionnaire flows per country on the platform.
  3. Where the platform could not handle a regional requirement out of the box, wrote the technical specifications for engineering to extend it.

Where the platform couldn't do it out of the box, I wrote the spec so engineering could.

What changed

  • 20+ countries configured on a single BPMN platform for the bank.
  • Client-measured improvement in KYC cycle time.
