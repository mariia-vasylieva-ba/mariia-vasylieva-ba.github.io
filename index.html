About

Hello, I'm Mariia.

I'm a Senior Business Analyst who works at the point where engineering, operations, and product meet — the messy middle where things either get coordinated or quietly fall apart. Over 10+ years in fintech, payments, and enterprise tech, that's where I've found I'm most useful: turning unclear, fast-moving situations into systems and processes that hold up after I've moved on.

Most of my favourite work isn't glamorous. It's the disaster-recovery guide that becomes the basis for how a platform gets tested. The support-load analysis that frees an engineering team from drowning in Slack requests. The planning system that finally lets leadership see what's actually achievable. I care less about producing documents and more about whether something genuinely changed because I was there.

I also rebuilt my career from the ground up after moving to the UK, which, it turns out, is decent training for the job. Starting over in a new country teaches you to read a room quickly, ask the questions that feel obvious but aren't, and stay calm when nothing is yet familiar.

Outside work I'm usually elbow-deep in something hands-on. I bake sourdough, I'm slowly renovating my home myself, and I read a lot — 64 books last year, mostly modern prose, sci-fi and fantasy. There are also two cats who supervise all of the above, with mixed reviews.

What it's like working with me

I'm the person who stays calm when things get noisy. When a project is mid-chaos — unclear ownership, competing priorities, everyone talking past each other — that's usually when I'm most useful, not least.

A few things people tend to say about working with me:

  • I ask the awkward question early. Usually some version of "what problem are we actually solving here?" before we've spent three sprints building the wrong thing.
  • I think in systems, not just tasks. I can't help spotting the messy process, the missing source of truth, the thing that'll break at scale — and then quietly fixing it so it stays fixed.
  • I work credibly with engineers. I can read the API spec, follow the architecture conversation, and write the technical specification myself, so I'm a bridge, not a translation bottleneck.
  • I'm easy to work with. Analytical doesn't have to mean intense. I'm friendly, I keep things calm, and I'd rather solve a problem alongside you than hand it over a wall.
